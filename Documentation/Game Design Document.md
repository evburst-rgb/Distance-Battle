# Distance Battle
## Every Meter Matters.
### Created by EvBurst

---

# Overview

Distance Battle is a custom Rocket League game mode where players must complete every distance from **1 meter through 200 meters**, avoid the **Dead Zone (201–249 meters)**, and finish by earning a **250+ meter** shot.

Every distance is earned individually and permanently saved until the player chooses to reset their progress.

---

# Objective

Complete every distance:

1–200

Then finish with:

250+

The first player to complete every objective wins.

---

# Core Gameplay Loop

1. Player lines up a shot.
2. Player strikes the ball.
3. Ball crosses the Start Gate.
4. Distance tracking begins.
5. Camera follows the ball.
6. Ball lands.
7. Flight distance is calculated.
8. Distance is rounded.
9. Progress board updates.
10. Camera returns.
11. Ball resets.
12. Repeat.

---

# Distance Rules

Tracking begins when the ball crosses the Start Gate.

Tracking ends on the first ground contact.

Rolling distance is ignored.

Bounce distance is ignored.

Only airborne flight distance counts.

---

# Rounding Rules

Flight Distance is rounded to the nearest whole meter.

Examples:

42.49 = 42

42.50 = 43

199.51 = 200

---

# Dead Zone

The Dead Zone covers:

201–249 meters

Shots landing inside the Dead Zone do not award progress.

The Dead Zone exists to increase the challenge of reaching the final objective.

---

# Final Objective

250+

Any officially rounded distance of 250 meters or greater completes the final objective.

Distances above 250 are not tracked separately.

---

# Start Gate

The Start Gate is positioned at 0 meters.

Players cannot cross the gate.

The ball is the only object permitted to cross.

When the ball crosses:

• Tracking begins
• Gate animation plays
• Audio plays
• Camera transitions

---

# Camera

Default camera remains behind the player.

Once the ball crosses the Start Gate:

• Camera smoothly follows the ball.

When the ball lands:

• Camera remains on the ball for approximately two seconds.

After the celebration:

• Camera smoothly returns behind the player.

---

# Progress Board

The board begins with:

1–200

250+

Every completed objective disappears.

Remaining objectives collapse together.

Progress is automatically updated.

---

# Save System

Player progress is automatically saved.

Completed objectives remain unlocked between sessions.

Players may manually reset all progress.

---

# Arena

Half-size arena.

Player Zone behind the Start Gate.

Distance markers:

10m spacing through 200m.

Large Dead Zone section.

250+ Finish Line.

Open field.

300m Branding Wall.
